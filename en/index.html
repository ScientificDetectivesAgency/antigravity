Hi, I'm Karime González-Zuccolotto.

I specialize in the design and development of geographic information systems (GIS) and geospatial analysis oriented towards applied research. I view analytical tools not as an end in themselves, but as research devices that allow us to formulate questions, explore patterns, and generate knowledge about complex phenomena. My main interest lies in risk, understood in a broad and integrative manner, including both natural hazards and anthropogenic processes, such as armed conflicts. Through the use of GIS, spatial data, and satellite imagery, I seek to understand how these hazards manifest in the territory and what their social, environmental, and spatial effects are. Specifically, I am interested in monitoring war environments through Earth observation as a way to document processes that are difficult to observe directly and to provide evidence that helps interpret and communicate their impacts on society. I am truly passionate about what I do, and I care deeply that data is used with care and purpose, especially when it can help better understand problems that affect society.
